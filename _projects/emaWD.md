details_en: |
  This was a Tongji University undergraduate innovation training
  (结题) project: using emaWD to simulate and improve manual work in
  intelligent manufacturing, with a case study of reducer manual
  assembly. The approach centers human work—abstracting the process
  into simulation, validating design choices before costly physical
  trials, and improving comfort and line efficiency. Work covered
  workstation layout and hardware sizing (e.g. adjustable-height
  stations, 900–950 mm work height, “heavy below, light above”
  material placement), biomechanics and posture (RULA/OWAS, joint load
  and action economy), and takt / bottleneck analysis (task split,
  merge, and sequence). Reported metrics include lower walking and
  bending risk indices, RULA from 6 to 2, and about 9.2% production
  time reduction with more stable pacing.

details_zh: |
  同济大学大学生创新训练项目结题工作：在智能化制造与人工作业无法分离的背景下，以 emaWD 为工具，对一款减速器的人工装配进行仿真、分析与改进，目标是在启用流水线与工位前发现问题、提升产线效能与工人舒适度。

  研究以人工工作为核心，通过仿真分模块从工位布局、人体姿势与节拍分析三方面开展优化，并结合人因学与健康科学对作业环境、动作与身体负荷作直观评价。

  工位侧优化设备、物料高度与布局（如站点式布局、行走与弯腰等风险指征）；姿势侧采用 RULA、OWAS 等评估并调整工位、工具高度与作业流程；节拍侧拆分瓶颈任务、合并细分任务、调整装配顺序以均衡负荷。

  结题中总结了研究论文、仿真与模型搭建方面的成果，并给出优化前后量化对比（如 RULA 评分下降、生产周期约 9.2% 提升等）。
